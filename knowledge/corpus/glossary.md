# Running Glossary — Terms Explained Simply

## Training Terms

### Pace & Speed
- **Pace:** Time per kilometer (or mile). E.g., 5:30/km means 5 minutes 30 seconds per km.
- **Splits:** Pace for each km segment. "Negative split" = second half faster than first.
- **Cadence:** Steps per minute (spm). Typical: 160-185 spm.
- **Tempo:** A run at "comfortably hard" pace — you can speak in short phrases only.
- **Threshold:** The fastest pace you can sustain for ~60 minutes. Also called lactate threshold pace.
- **VO2max pace:** The pace at which you reach maximum oxygen consumption. Roughly your 3K-5K race pace.
- **Race pace:** The pace you plan to run your target race at.
- **Easy pace:** Conversational pace. Feels almost too slow. Where 80% of training should happen.

### Volume & Load
- **Mileage / Volume:** Total km run per week.
- **10% Rule:** Never increase weekly volume by more than 10% per week.
- **ACWR:** Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio. Compares recent load (7 days) to average (28 days). Safe zone: 0.8-1.3.
- **TSS:** Training Stress Score. Normalizes load by intensity. 100 TSS = 1 hour at threshold.
- **Deload:** A planned reduced-volume week (usually every 3-4 weeks) to allow adaptation.
- **Taper:** The deliberate reduction of training volume before a race (1-3 weeks depending on race distance).

### Workout Types
- **Easy run:** Low intensity, conversational, builds aerobic base.
- **Long run:** The longest run of the week. Builds endurance. Usually 25-35% of weekly volume.
- **Tempo run:** 20-40 minutes at threshold pace. Improves lactate clearance.
- **Intervals:** Hard efforts (3-5 min) with recovery periods. Builds VO2max.
- **Repetitions (Reps):** Very short, very fast efforts (200-400m) with full recovery. Builds speed/economy.
- **Fartlek:** "Speed play." Unstructured mix of paces within a run.
- **Progression run:** Start easy, finish fast. Teaches negative splitting.
- **Recovery run:** Shorter and slower than easy. Active recovery between hard days.
- **Strides:** 6-8 x 20-second accelerations at fast (not sprint) pace with full recovery. Done after easy runs.

### Periodization
- **Base phase:** High volume, low intensity. Building the aerobic engine.
- **Build phase:** Introduction of quality sessions. Volume stabilizes or slightly increases.
- **Peak/sharpen phase:** Highest intensity percentage. Race-specific preparation.
- **Taper phase:** Volume drops, intensity maintained. Arrive fresh for race.
- **Mesocycle:** A 3-4 week training block with a specific focus.
- **Macrocycle:** The full training period from start to race day (12-24 weeks typically).
- **Microcycle:** One week of training.

## Physiology Terms

### Energy & Fitness
- **VO2max:** Maximum oxygen your body can use during exercise (ml/kg/min). Higher = fitter aerobically.
- **Lactate threshold (LT2):** The intensity above which lactate accumulates faster than it clears. Running above this for too long = inevitable slowdown.
- **Aerobic threshold (LT1):** The intensity at which lactate first rises above resting levels. Marathon pace is near LT1.
- **Running economy:** How much oxygen you use at a given pace. Less = more efficient = faster.
- **VDOT:** Jack Daniels' fitness index derived from race performance. Higher = fitter.
- **Age grading:** Adjusting performance for age/gender. Allows fair comparison across ages.

### Heart & Body
- **Max HR:** Your heart's maximum beats per minute during all-out effort. Decreases ~1 bpm/year.
- **Resting HR:** Heart rate upon waking. Lower = generally fitter. Elite: 35-50 bpm.
- **HRV (Heart Rate Variability):** Variation between heartbeats. Higher HRV = better recovered.
- **Cardiac drift:** Heart rate gradually increasing during a run despite constant pace (due to dehydration, heat, fatigue).
- **Stroke volume:** Blood pumped per heartbeat. Increases with training (why resting HR drops).

### Injuries & Recovery
- **DOMS:** Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness. The ache 24-48h after a hard/new workout. Normal.
- **Stress fracture:** A small crack in bone from repetitive impact. NOT a muscle issue. Requires rest + imaging.
- **Plantar fasciitis:** Inflammation of the thick tissue on the sole of the foot. Heel/arch pain, worst in morning.
- **IT Band Syndrome:** Pain on outer knee from friction of the iliotibial band. Common in runners.
- **Runner's knee:** Pain around/behind the kneecap (patellofemoral pain). Usually from weak quads/glutes.
- **Achilles tendinopathy:** Degeneration of the Achilles tendon. NOT inflammation. Needs loading (not rest).
- **Shin splints:** Pain along the inner shin (medial tibial stress syndrome). Usually from too much too soon.
- **RICE:** Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation. The classic first-aid protocol for acute injury.
- **Eccentric exercise:** Muscle lengthens under load (e.g., lowering a calf raise slowly). Best for tendon healing.

## Race & Competition Terms

### Distances
- **5K:** 5 kilometers (3.1 miles). Takes 15-40 minutes for most runners.
- **10K:** 10 kilometers (6.2 miles). Takes 30-70 minutes.
- **Half Marathon:** 21.1 km (13.1 miles). Takes 1:15 to 3:00+ hours.
- **Marathon:** 42.195 km (26.2 miles). Takes 2:00 to 6:00+ hours.
- **Ultra:** Anything beyond marathon distance (50K, 100K, 100 miles).
- **Parkrun:** Free, timed 5K held every Saturday morning worldwide.

### Race Terminology
- **PB/PR:** Personal Best / Personal Record. Your fastest time at a distance.
- **Negative split:** Running the second half of a race faster than the first.
- **The wall:** Glycogen depletion in a marathon (usually around 30-35 km). Pace collapses.
- **DNF:** Did Not Finish. Dropping out of a race.
- **DNS:** Did Not Start. Registered but didn't show.
- **Corral:** Starting area in a race, usually seeded by expected pace.
- **Aid station:** On-course water/fuel/medical stop.
- **BQ:** Boston Qualifying time. The time standard needed to enter the Boston Marathon.
- **Gun time vs Chip time:** Gun time = from starting gun. Chip time = from when YOU cross the start mat.

## Nutrition Terms

### Fueling
- **Glycogen:** Stored carbohydrate in muscles and liver. Primary fuel for moderate-to-hard running.
- **Fat oxidation:** Using body fat for energy. Dominant at low intensity. Trainable.
- **Carb loading:** Increasing carb intake 2-3 days before a race to maximize glycogen stores.
- **Bonking / hitting the wall:** Running out of glycogen. Pace collapses. Prevention: fuel during long events.
- **GI distress:** Stomach issues during running (cramping, nausea, diarrhea). Common with untrained fueling.

### Supplements & Conditions
- **RED-S:** Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport. Under-fueling → hormonal disruption, bone loss, performance decline.
- **Female Athlete Triad:** Low energy availability + menstrual dysfunction + low bone density. Now part of RED-S.
- **Ferritin:** Iron stores in the body. Runners need >30 ng/mL (ideally >50). Low ferritin = fatigue.
- **Caffeine:** Legal performance enhancer. 3-6mg/kg improves endurance 2-4%.
- **Electrolytes:** Minerals (sodium, potassium, magnesium) lost in sweat. Need replacing in long/hot efforts.
- **ORS:** Oral Rehydration Solution. Salt + sugar + water. Better than plain water for post-exercise rehydration.

## Gear Terms

### Shoes
- **Stack height:** Thickness of the midsole. Higher = more cushion but less ground feel.
- **Drop:** Height difference between heel and forefoot (mm). Traditional: 10-12mm. Minimal: 0-4mm.
- **Carbon plate:** Stiff plate in racing shoes that adds propulsion. 4% performance benefit (Nike research).
- **Daily trainer:** Durable shoe for most training (300-500g, 8-12mm drop).
- **Racing flat / super shoe:** Lightweight, carbon-plated, max 200-250g. For racing only.
- **Rotation:** Using 2-3 different shoes to vary loading patterns and extend shoe lifespan.

### Technology
- **GPS watch:** Tracks distance, pace, HR, route, cadence.
- **Chest strap:** Most accurate HR monitoring (optical wrist sensors lag and err at high HR).
- **Stryd/power meter:** Measures running power in watts. Useful for hilly terrain pacing.
- **HRV app:** Measures heart rate variability daily for recovery monitoring (HRV4Training, EliteHRV).
