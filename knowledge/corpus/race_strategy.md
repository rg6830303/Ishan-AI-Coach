# Race Strategy, Pacing & Taper

## Pacing Strategies

### Even Pacing (Recommended for Most)
Run the same pace from start to finish. The most energy-efficient strategy. Requires discipline in the first km (always feels too slow). Ideal for: 10K, half marathon, marathon for experienced runners.

### Negative Split
Run the second half faster than the first. The holy grail of racing. Requires starting conservatively (5-10 sec/km slower than goal pace for first 3-5km) then gradually accelerating. Best for: experienced runners who know their body. Hardest to execute but lowest risk of blowing up.

### Positive Split (What Usually Happens)
Start too fast, slow down in the second half. This is what 95% of amateur runners do accidentally. Every second too fast in the first half costs 2-3 seconds in the second half (exponential relationship). The first km feeling "easy" at goal pace is a trap — adrenaline masks true effort.

### Progressive Pace (for Training)
Start easy, finish fast. Great for long runs (easy for 75%, moderate for 15%, strong for 10%). Teaches finishing speed and mental toughness. NOT a race strategy — too conservative for competition.

### The 5K/10K Difference
- **5K:** Can afford slight positive split (start 3-5% fast, hang on). Hurt locker tolerance matters.
- **10K:** Even pace or very slight negative. Too fast first 2km = severe fade after 7km.
- **Half:** Even pace critical. First 5km always feel too slow if correct.
- **Marathon:** Even or negative only. Positive split = the wall at 32km.

## The Taper

### What Taper Does
Reduces fatigue while maintaining fitness. Net effect: 2-6% performance improvement. The taper works even when it FEELS wrong (you'll feel sluggish, heavy, anxious — this is normal).

### Duration by Race
- **5K:** 5-7 days (minimal taper needed)
- **10K:** 7-10 days
- **Half Marathon:** 10-14 days
- **Marathon:** 2-3 weeks (the classic 3-week marathon taper)

### How to Taper
- **Volume:** Reduce by 40-60% total. Biggest cuts to easy runs and long run.
- **Intensity:** MAINTAIN. Keep 1-2 short quality sessions (shorter duration, same pace).
- **Frequency:** Keep the same (or reduce by 1 day max). Don't go from 6 days to 2.
- **Week before race:** 2-3 short shakeout runs with race-pace strides. Rest day 2 days before race.

### Taper Mistakes
- Panicking and doing a "test run" at race pace (wastes energy, doesn't build fitness)
- Complete rest for 5+ days (lose sharpness and feel heavy)
- Trying a new food/supplement/shoe during taper week
- Overcompensating reduced training with overfeeding (small carb increase is fine, not a food festival)
- Catching up on missed sleep by sleeping 12+ hours (disrupts circadian rhythm)

### The Taper Tantrum
It's real. Reduced training → reduced endorphins → irritability, self-doubt, heavy legs. This is normal and a SIGN THE TAPER IS WORKING. Fatigue is dissipating and it feels weird because you're used to training tired.

## Race-Day Logistics

### Night Before
- Lay out everything: shoes, race outfit, bib, gels, watch, sunscreen
- Set 2 alarms (phone + backup)
- Eat carb-heavy dinner by 7pm (not 10pm — digestion needs time)
- No alcohol (dehydrates, disrupts sleep)
- Bed by 9-10pm (you won't sleep great — that's okay, it's 2-nights-before sleep that matters)

### Morning Of
- Wake 3+ hours before gun time
- Familiar breakfast (practiced in training): toast, banana, oatmeal
- Hydrate: 500ml water with breakfast, stop 60 min before start
- Bathroom visit: coffee helps timing
- Arrive 60-90 min before start (logistics, warmup, corral)
- Warmup for 5K/10K: 10-15 min jog + strides. For marathon: 5 min walk + light jog.

### First Kilometer
The most important km of any race. Adrenaline + crowd + fresh legs = running 20-30 sec/km too fast. EVERY experienced runner knows: if the first km feels comfortable at goal pace, you are too fast. It should feel boringly slow. Check your watch at 500m and adjust.

### Managing the Middle
- Km 5-15 of a half / km 10-30 of a marathon: the "grinding zone"
- Break it into segments: "just get to the next aid station"
- Maintain cadence even when pace drifts
- Use other runners to draft (3-5% energy saving running behind someone)
- Process aid stations efficiently (grab, pinch cup, sip, discard)

### The Finish
- **5K/10K:** All-out final 400m is fine (short enough to sustain)
- **Half marathon:** Start pushing final 2-3km if you have reserves
- **Marathon:** If you have anything left at 35km, go. If not, just maintain.
- Lean at the line (chip timing is on your shoe but photo finishes use torso)

## Racing in Heat

### Pace Adjustment
- 20-25°C: Slow 2-5% from cool-weather goal pace
- 25-30°C: Slow 5-10%
- 30°C+: Slow 10-15% or defer to effort-based running
- Humidity amplifies heat effect (sweat can't evaporate)

### Heat Strategy
- Start conservative (even more than usual)
- Pour water on head/neck at every aid station
- Ice in hat/sports bra if available
- Drink to thirst + electrolytes every 15-20 min
- Accept the adjusted goal — fighting heat costs more energy than the time you "save"

### When to DNF
- Confusion or disorientation
- Stopping sweating despite heat (thermoregulation failure)
- Visual disturbance
- Vomiting that doesn't stop
- Core temp feeling: if you genuinely feel "wrong" — trust that signal

## Racing in Cold/Rain

### Cold (<5°C)
- Warm up inside/in shelter until 10 min before start
- Wear disposable outer layer (bin bag works) until gun time, then discard
- Gloves + ear cover (lose 30% heat from extremities/head)
- You'll warm up within 5-10 min — don't overdress for racing
- Vaseline on exposed skin (wind protection)

### Rain
- Vaseline on chafe-prone areas (nipples, inner thighs, underarms)
- Hat with brim (keeps rain out of eyes)
- Don't wear cotton (gets heavy, causes chafing)
- Shoes will get wet — accept it. Wet feet + friction = blisters: use anti-blister socks or tape

## Mental Race Strategy

### Chunking
Don't think about the full distance. Break into segments: next km marker, next aid station, next landmark. A marathon is not 42km — it's 8 segments of 5km + a 2km finish.

### Mantras
Pre-choose 2-3 words for when it hurts: "smooth and strong," "relax and roll," "I trained for this." Simple, rhythmic, believable.

### Association vs Dissociation
- **Association:** Focus on body signals (pace, form, breathing). Use in early race to prevent going too fast.
- **Dissociation:** Distract from discomfort (music, counting, scenery). Use in the grinding middle.
- **Elite strategy:** Associate during hard moments (adjust form, check pace), dissociate during steady state.

### The Pain Cave (final 20%)
- Pain is temporary, finishing is forever
- Switch to pure process: next step, next breath
- Recall your training: "I've done the work"
- Find someone ahead and reel them in (competition narrows focus)
- Smile — forced smiling reduces perceived effort by 2% (research-supported)
