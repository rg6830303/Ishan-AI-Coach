# Running Nutrition, Fueling & Hydration

## Daily Nutrition for Runners

### Macronutrient Guidelines
- **Carbohydrates:** 5-7g/kg/day for moderate training, 7-10g/kg for heavy training, 10-12g/kg for carb loading. Carbs are the primary fuel for quality running.
- **Protein:** 1.4-1.7g/kg/day for runners. Essential for muscle repair. Spread across meals (20-40g per serving). Higher end during heavy training or injury recovery.
- **Fat:** 1.0-1.5g/kg/day minimum. Essential for hormones, joint health, fat-soluble vitamins. Don't go below 20% of calories.

### Meal Timing
- **Pre-run (2-3h before):** High carb, moderate protein, low fat/fiber. Example: oatmeal with banana, toast with peanut butter.
- **Pre-run (30-60 min before):** Simple carbs only. Banana, energy bar, white toast with honey.
- **Post-run (within 30-60 min):** 3:1 carb:protein ratio. 1-1.2g/kg carbs + 20-30g protein. Recovery window is real for glycogen restoration.
- **Evening before long run:** Carb-heavy dinner. Pasta, rice, potatoes with lean protein.

### The Indian Runner's Plate
Culturally relevant fueling options:
- **Pre-run:** Banana + chai (no milk), idli, upma, poha
- **Post-run:** Curd rice, dal-rice, paneer paratha, protein smoothie with milk/curd
- **Race fuel:** Chikki (jaggery-peanut), dates, glucose biscuits, homemade energy balls
- **Daily protein sources:** Dal, paneer, curd, eggs, chicken, soy chunks, sprouts, whey

## Race-Day Fueling

### Pre-Race Nutrition
- **Night before:** Familiar carb-heavy meal. Nothing new. 8-12g/kg carbs if carb-loaded.
- **Morning (3h before):** 2-4g/kg carbs. Toast/banana/oatmeal. Avoid high fiber/fat/dairy.
- **30 min before:** 30g simple carbs (gel, sports drink, banana).

### During-Race Fueling
- **Under 60 min:** Water only. No fuel needed.
- **60-90 min:** Water + 30g/hr carbs optional (sports drink).
- **90+ min:** 60g/hr carbs (2 gels/hr or equivalent). Train this in long runs first!
- **2.5+ hours (marathon):** 60-90g/hr carbs. Mix glucose + fructose for better absorption. Practice religiously — gut training is essential.

### Gel/Fuel Strategy for Marathons
- Start fueling at 30 min (before you feel you need it)
- Every 20-25 min thereafter (not every 45 min — too late by then)
- Always take with water (not sports drink — double sugar overload)
- Practice exact brand/flavor/timing in training long runs
- If you can't tolerate gels: sports drink, chews, real food (dates, banana pieces)

## Hydration

### Daily Hydration
- Base: 35-40ml/kg/day (2.5-3L for a 70kg runner)
- Add 500-750ml per hour of running
- Monitor: urine should be pale yellow. Dark = dehydrated. Clear = overhydrating.

### During Running
- **Under 60 min (cool weather):** Drink to thirst. Usually 400-600ml/hr.
- **Over 60 min or hot weather:** 400-800ml/hr depending on sweat rate.
- **Sweat rate test:** Weigh before and after 1h run. Each kg lost = 1L sweat. Replace 150% over next 2-4h.

### Electrolytes
- Sodium is the key electrolyte lost in sweat (500-1500mg/L of sweat)
- For runs >90 min or heavy sweaters: electrolyte drink/tabs
- Post-run: salted food, ORS, coconut water with salt
- Hyponatremia risk: drinking too much plain water during long events dilutes sodium. Use sports drink for marathons.

### Heat and Hydration
- Pre-hydrate: 500ml 2h before running in heat
- Drink to thirst, don't force
- Sip throughout, don't gulp 500ml at once
- Post-run in heat: extra 500-1000ml over normal replacement

## Iron & Running

### Why Runners Are At Risk
- Foot-strike hemolysis (red blood cell destruction from impact)
- GI blood loss from running (especially in heat)
- Sweat iron loss
- Female runners: menstrual iron loss adds to running losses
- Vegetarian/vegan runners: lower bioavailability from plant sources

### Symptoms of Iron Deficiency
- Unusual fatigue during easy runs
- Elevated HR at usual pace
- Heavy legs that don't improve with rest
- Shortness of breath disproportionate to effort
- Brittle nails, pale skin, cold extremities

### Iron Guidelines
- Check ferritin annually (runners should target >30 ng/mL, ideally >50)
- Heme iron (meat) is 2-3x better absorbed than non-heme (plant)
- Vitamin C with iron-rich meals enhances absorption
- Avoid tea/coffee within 1h of iron-rich meals (tannins block absorption)
- Supplement ONLY with blood test confirmation — excess iron is toxic

## RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport)

### What It Is
When energy intake is chronically insufficient for the demands of training + daily life. Previously called "female athlete triad" but affects all genders. Under-fueling causes:
- Impaired performance (opposite of what the athlete intended)
- Bone stress injuries (low estrogen/testosterone → bone loss)
- Hormonal disruption (amenorrhea in females, low testosterone in males)
- Immune suppression, mood disorders, GI issues

### Warning Signs
- Unintentional weight loss during training buildup
- Missing periods (females) or low libido (males)
- Recurring stress fractures or bone injuries
- Performance plateau despite increased training
- Chronic fatigue, frequent illness
- Obsessive calorie counting or food restriction

### For the Coach (AI)
- NEVER prescribe calorie deficits for "race weight"
- Flag if a runner reports restrictive eating + high volume
- Recommend professional assessment (sports dietitian) if RED-S suspected
- Include medical disclaimer: "This is not medical advice. Please consult a healthcare provider."
- If someone reports missed periods + running: flag immediately

## Caffeine and Performance

### Evidence
Caffeine improves endurance performance by 2-4% (well-established). Works via adenosine receptor blocking (reduces perceived effort) and improved fat oxidation.

### Practical Guidelines
- Dose: 3-6mg/kg body weight, 45-60 min before race/hard session
- For 70kg runner: 210-420mg (roughly 2-4 cups of coffee)
- Don't introduce on race day — practice in training
- Habitual users may need to reduce intake 5-7 days before race for full effect
- Avoid after 2pm (sleep quality is recovery quality)

### Timing in Marathon
- 200mg caffeine pre-race (in breakfast/coffee)
- Caffeinated gel at 60-90 min and again at 2h
- Total: don't exceed 6mg/kg during the event

## Weight Management for Runners

### Healthy Approach
- Small deficit (200-300 cal/day) during base phase ONLY, never during build/peak
- Rate: 0.5kg/week maximum to preserve muscle
- Fuel training fully — cut from non-training hours
- Never restrict on training days or within 2h of running

### What NOT To Do
- Run fasted to "burn fat" (impairs quality, increases injury risk)
- Cut carbs while training hard (glycogen depletion = poor performance + injury)
- Chase race weight at the expense of health
- Restrict during peak/race phase (you need fuel for performance)

### Body Composition vs Performance
Every kg of body weight costs ~2.5 seconds per km at 5K pace. But: under-fueling to lose weight reduces power output by MORE than the weight savings help. There is an optimum — usually found at 18-22% body fat for female runners, 8-14% for males. Below this: hormonal disruption, bone loss, performance decline.
