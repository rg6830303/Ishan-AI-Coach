# Running in Different Environments — Heat, Cold, Altitude, Rain

## Heat Training & Racing

### Physiological Impact of Heat
- Core temp rises 1°C per 5-8 min of hard running without cooling
- VO2max decreases ~1.5% per 1°C core temp above 38°C
- Heart rate increases 5-10 bpm at same pace in heat (cardiac drift)
- Sweat rate: 0.5-2.5 L/hour depending on fitness, acclimatization, humidity
- Performance loss: 2-10% for marathon in hot conditions (vs cool)

### WBGT (Wet Bulb Globe Temperature) Guidelines
| WBGT | Risk Level | Recommendation |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| < 18°C | Low | Normal training/racing |
| 18-23°C | Moderate | Reduce intensity slightly, hydrate |
| 23-28°C | High | Slow 5-10%, extra hydration + electrolytes |
| 28-30°C | Very High | Easy running only, avoid intervals |
| > 30°C | Extreme | Consider postponing or indoor alternative |

### Heat Acclimatization Protocol
**Duration:** 10-14 days of heat exposure (5+ sessions)
**Method:** Run in heat for 45-75 min at easy-moderate effort. NOT intervals.
**Adaptations gained:**
- Earlier onset of sweating
- Increased sweat rate (more efficient cooling)
- Lower core temp at same pace
- Expanded plasma volume (better blood flow)
- Reduced heart rate at same effort
- Lower perceived effort at same pace

**Practical approach for Indian runners:**
- India's summer (April-June): natural acclimatization opportunity
- Train in the hottest part of the day 3-4x/week for adaptation
- Race prep: if racing in heat, train in heat. If racing in cool conditions, train early morning.
- Acclimatization is LOST within 1 week of cool conditions — needs maintenance

### Pre-Cooling Strategies
- Ice vest / cold towels on neck 15-30 min before warm race
- Cold water/slushie 30 min before start (lowers core temp by 0.3-0.5°C)
- Cold shower before heading to start line
- Benefit: delays time to critical core temp → more time at target pace

### Mid-Race Cooling
- Water over head/neck at every aid station (not just drinking)
- Ice in hat, sports bra, or held in hands
- Sponges on wrists and neck (high blood flow areas)
- Slow through shaded sections, push on exposed sections (thermal management)

## Cold Weather Running

### Physiological Impact
- Airways constrict (exercise-induced bronchoconstriction risk)
- Blood diverted from extremities to core (cold fingers/toes)
- Muscles less elastic → higher strain injury risk if not warmed up
- VO2max NOT significantly affected by cold (unlike heat)
- Performance: marginally IMPROVED in cold (easier to dump heat)

### Layering System
**Layer 1 (Base):** Moisture-wicking synthetic or merino wool. NEVER cotton.
**Layer 2 (Mid):** Insulating layer (fleece/thermal). Only needed below 0°C.
**Layer 3 (Shell):** Windproof. Only needed in wind/rain + cold combo.

### Temperature Guidelines
| Temp | Layers | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 10-15°C | T-shirt + shorts | "Ideal running weather" |
| 5-10°C | Long sleeve or light jacket + shorts/tights | Gloves optional |
| 0-5°C | Thermal top + tights + gloves + headband | Cover ears |
| -5 to 0°C | 2 layers + tights + gloves + hat + buff | Cover all skin |
| < -5°C | Full winter kit + face cover | Limit duration to 45-60 min |

### Cold-Specific Rules
- Warmup INSIDE before heading out (5-10 min dynamic movement)
- Run into the wind FIRST (so return trip is wind-assisted when sweaty)
- Don't overdress — you should feel slightly cool in the first 5 min (body warms quickly)
- Carry phone + emergency layer for long runs (if you stop, you cool FAST)
- Watch for ice: shorten stride, increase cadence, flat foot landing on slick surfaces
- Post-run: change out of wet clothes IMMEDIATELY (hypothermia risk from evaporative cooling)

### Frostbite Prevention
- Most vulnerable: ears, nose tip, fingers, toes
- Windchill below -15°C: cover all exposed skin
- If numbness progresses to pain → GET INDOORS
- Never run alone in extreme cold (if you slip/fall and can't move → emergency)

## Altitude Training & Racing

### Acute Effects (First 1-7 days)
- Reduced O2 pressure → higher ventilation → faster dehydration
- VO2max drops immediately (~7% per 1000m above 1500m)
- Heart rate elevated at same pace
- Sleep disruption (Cheyne-Stokes breathing at night)
- Headache, fatigue, nausea possible (Acute Mountain Sickness)

### Chronic Adaptations (2-4 weeks)
- Increased EPO production → more red blood cells → better O2 carrying
- Improved mitochondrial efficiency
- Enhanced capillary density in muscles
- Increased ventilatory efficiency
- Benefits persist 2-3 weeks after descending

### Live High, Train Low (LHTL)
The gold standard protocol:
- LIVE at 2000-2500m (stimulus for EPO/RBC production)
- TRAIN at <1200m (maintain training intensity/quality)
- Duration: 3-4 weeks minimum
- Not always practical — simulated altitude (tents, masks) is an alternative with weaker evidence

### Racing at Altitude
**Option A:** Arrive 2+ weeks early and acclimatize
**Option B:** Arrive within 12-24 hours of the race (before AMS sets in, race on sea-level fitness)
**Worst option:** Arrive 2-5 days before (AMS peaks, no adaptation yet)

**Pace adjustment for altitude racing:**
- 1500m elevation: slow 3-5%
- 2000m: slow 5-8%
- 2500m: slow 8-12%
- 3000m+: slow 12-20% (ultra/mountain running territory)

### Indian Context
- Many Indian races at altitude: Ladakh Marathon (3500m), Sikkim runs, hill station races
- Training in Delhi/Mumbai (sea level) → racing at 2000m+ requires preparation
- Minimum: arrive 3+ days before. Ideal: 10-14 days. Or arrive day-before.
- Hydrate extra (altitude + dry air = fast dehydration)

## Rain Running

### Gear Priorities
1. **Hat with brim** — keeps rain out of eyes (more important than any jacket)
2. **Anti-chafe:** Vaseline on nipples, inner thighs, underarms, bra line, between toes
3. **Synthetic/wool clothing** — NEVER cotton (gets heavy, causes horrific chafing)
4. **Shoes:** Accept they'll get wet. Lightweight mesh dries faster than waterproof shoes (which trap water once it gets in over the ankle)
5. **Waterproof jacket:** Only if cold + rain combo. Otherwise just get wet (jacket traps heat).

### Performance Impact
- Wet surface: slightly higher injury risk (slipping). Shorten stride on slick surfaces.
- Added weight from wet clothes: negligible (200-300g)
- Cooling effect: can be BENEFICIAL in hot rain (natural cooling)
- Psychological: running in rain builds mental toughness. Most competitors DNS in bad weather — fewer people to beat.

### Post-Rain Care
- Change immediately after — don't sit in wet kit
- Dry shoes: stuff with newspaper, NOT near heater (warps midsole)
- Check feet: wet + friction = blisters. Treat early. Tape hot spots.
- Wash muddy shoes with cold water only (hot water degrades adhesives)

## Air Quality & Pollution

### AQI (Air Quality Index) Guidelines for Running
| AQI | Category | Running Recommendation |
|-----|----------|----------------------|
| 0-50 | Good | Normal training |
| 51-100 | Moderate | Sensitive individuals reduce intensity |
| 101-150 | Unhealthy for sensitive | Reduce duration/intensity for all |
| 151-200 | Unhealthy | Indoor exercise only (treadmill, gym) |
| 200+ | Very unhealthy/hazardous | No outdoor exercise |

### Indian Cities — Practical Reality
- Delhi (Oct-Feb): AQI regularly 200-500. Move training indoors or very early morning.
- Mumbai/Chennai: Generally 50-150 (moderate). Morning training preferred.
- Bangalore: Generally good (30-80). Hill stations: excellent.
- Diwali week: Universally terrible across North India. Plan deload week.

### Pollution Mitigation
- Early morning (5-7 AM): lowest AQI of the day (before traffic, before heat lifts particles)
- Parks/greenery: 20-30% lower AQI than roadside
- N95 mask for commute, not for running (restricts breathing too much during exercise)
- Post-pollution-run: nasal saline rinse, stay hydrated (helps clear particles)
- Long-term: chronic exposure to AQI>100 reduces VO2max development. If you live in a polluted city, maximize indoor training or relocate key training blocks.

## Treadmill as Environment Solution

### When Treadmill Wins
- AQI > 150 (health protection)
- Temperature > 40°C or < -10°C (safety)
- Dark + unsafe route (personal safety)
- Precise interval control (no stoplights, no hills when unwanted)
- Injury recovery (controlled pace, cushioned surface)

### Treadmill Calibration for Outdoor Equivalent
- Set 1.0-1.5% incline to simulate air resistance (Jones & Doust, 1996)
- Perceived effort at same HR: slightly lower on treadmill (belt assistance + no wind)
- Neuromuscular pattern: slightly different (less hamstring activation, different propulsion)
- Don't train EXCLUSIVELY on treadmill — mix with outdoor for race-specific adaptation

### Treadmill Workouts That Work
- Easy runs: set pace and incline, zone out (podcast/music/TV)
- Intervals: precise pace control, auto-incline for progressive simulation
- Hill simulation: 5-8% incline repeats (steeper than most runners can find outdoors)
- Progression run: increase speed by 0.5 km/h every 5 min (teaches negative-split pacing)
- Marathon-pace long run: exact goal pace without terrain/traffic disruption
